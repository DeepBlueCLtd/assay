# Research note 13 — Verdict legibility & the argument surface: making the mapping, the consequence, and the challenge inspectable

Review slice S-F (SPEC-25) · per ASSAY-DEC-11 · 2026-07-16
Prompts (review `docs/reviews/2026-07-14-jipoe-c2-process-review.md` §3.1, §3.3, mockups M3/M4/M8/M10, addendum §10 slice S-F): five legibility moves over machinery ASSAY already has. Four of them — the public verdict legend (US1), relaxation cards v2 (US3), the challenge affordance (US4), per-role action menus (US5) — are **re-renderings of already-computed, already-banked outputs**; the presentation-audit discipline that governs them was decided in note 09 (§3, the band-pill audit) and needs no new gate. The **one** move that reads the world in a genuinely new way — the **consequence preview** (US2), "what would this write change, before I commit it" — is the DEC-11 subject of this note. This note decides the preview's honesty rules (§2–§5), and records for the audit trail why the other four are register-neutral presentation (§6–§7).

Output feeds SPEC-25 (`specs/025-verdict-legibility/`). Per DEC-5 the slice is a projection plus, for US2, one new **interaction class** — never a new engine (DEC-10), never a stored object, never a delta.

## 1. The frame — legibility is where a demonstrator drifts into theatre

The review's five moves share a shape: each answers a question a reader asks *at the point of reading a verdict* — "why this word?" (US1), "what happens if I commit this?" (US2), "what else does this candidate cost?" (US3), "what is this verdict leaning on?" (US4), "what am I allowed to do here?" (US5). None of them re-derives a number. That is the opportunity and the trap in one: legibility that *re-computes behind a friendly face* would be theatre — a second, softer engine drawn over the honest one. The binding discipline for the whole slice is therefore negative: **no legibility surface may compute a verdict, a margin, a ranking, or a sacrifice that the real services did not already compute.** Every surface here is a lens onto a banked output, or (US2) the real pipeline run visibly on a shadow.

Two literatures anchor the slice.

- **The Combat Results Table (CRT)** — the hex-and-counter wargame's public outcome table (Dunnigan, *The Complete Wargames Handbook*, 3rd ed. 2000; the table itself is as old as *Tactics II*, 1958). The CRT is the oldest public-outcome-table UI there is: the odds-ratio column, the die row, the result cell — the *mapping* from inputs to outcome laid open on the rulebook page, never a hidden judgement of the referee. ASSAY's four-stop verdict is exactly a CRT cell: a mapping from a margin band's sign to a word. US1 renders that table. (The CRT precedent also fixes what the legend must *not* be: it is not a probability — the wargame CRT is a lookup, and so is ours; the four stops are regions of a sign mapping, not a distribution over outcomes, DEC-15.)
- **Telegraphed consequence** — *Into the Breach* (Subset Games, 2018) makes every enemy's next action, and the exact grid effect of the player's move, visible *before* commitment; the player commits to a fully-previewed board. The design literature calls this "no hidden information, no undo needed" — the preview is honest because it is the same resolution engine the commit will run, not an estimate (see also the UX "telegraphing" pattern, and the chess "touch-move with full board visibility" analogue). US2 is that contract applied to knowledge work: arm a write, see the real verdict-matrix consequence computed by the live pipeline, then commit or cancel.
- **The challenge counter-literature** — the UK *Red Teaming Handbook* (Development, Concepts and Doctrine Centre, 3rd ed. 2021) warns that *lip-service challenge instils more confidence than none*: an "assumptions check" that surfaces nothing computed, or that lets the reader feel challenged without being challenged, is worse than absent. US4's binding consequence: the challenge affordance must surface **computed** sensitivity (the SPEC-11 ranking), never an authored "have you considered…" — and when nothing flips the verdict, it must say so plainly (an insensitive verdict is assurance, not a blank).

## 2. The consequence preview, decided — the shadow-state contract (US2, the DEC-11 subject)

**Decision.** A preview is *the real pipeline run over a shadow of the store*: a byte-for-byte fork of the committed state (object store, trace graph, delta log), on which the armed act is applied through **the same service methods** as a commit, and over which **the same snapshot** is driven. The preview's diff is the set difference of the value-keyed glow signatures between the committed snapshot and the shadow snapshot. Committing the armed act then applies exactly that act to the real state; cancelling discards the fork. Nothing about the shadow ever touches the committed store, trace, or delta log.

This is the honest choice among the alternatives, decided against each:

- **Estimate the diff** (predict which cells move without running the pipeline) — **rejected**. An estimate is a second model of the scorer; the moment it disagrees with the real re-score the preview has lied. DEC-10 forbids a second engine; the review's own framing ("honest because it is the live pipeline, not an estimate") forecloses it.
- **Mutate-then-revert the real store** (apply the act, snapshot, undo) — **rejected**. The store is append-only and content-addressed with *no delete* (SPEC-01, knowledge model §2): there is no revert. A `put` during preview would persist a version and a `contest`/`resolve` would publish a delta — the preview would leave a trace, violating "persists nothing, emits no delta, mutates no stamp" (FR-002). Immutability makes reverting impossible *and* makes forking cheap (§2.2), so the store's own design points at the fork.
- **A seam-level preview endpoint** (`POST /preview` returning a diff) — **deferred, not chosen**. It is a legitimate future contract (the spec flags it as a further candidate), but it would be a new seam movement type asserting a decision this slice has no mandate to assert. The client-side fork needs no seam change: it composes the existing service calls over a cloned store. v1 previews client-side; a seam contract is a later candidate if a second consumer needs it.

### 2.1 The five binding rules of the preview

1. **Same services, same firewall.** The armed act runs through `KnowledgeService.resolve/supersede/contest` (or, for a plan edit, `store.put` of a new immutable version) exactly as a commit does. The encoding firewall, the contested-never-compiles gate (G5), the confidence lint, the comparability guard all apply to the shadow. **A dishonest armed edit previews its refusal** (§2.3): the preview of a band edit that trips the encoding firewall *is* the refusal banner, shown in ghost form. This is the strongest honesty property of the whole slice — arming a dishonest write *teaches the firewall without a commit*.
2. **Nothing persisted, no delta, no stamp mutation.** The fork owns its own store/trace/delta log; the committed ones are never read-modified. After `preview → cancel`, `app.store.size`, the delta count, and every stamp are byte-identical to before arming (FR-002, testable).
3. **Previewed diff ≡ post-commit diff, byte-for-byte.** Because the fork runs *literally the same snapshot code* over a byte-identical clone, the set of glow units whose signature moves under the shadow act equals — element for element — the set that moves when the same act is committed and re-snapshotted. G1 (determinism over inputs) guarantees it; a test asserts it (SC-002). This is the property that makes the preview *the* preview and not *a* preview: **the ghost is what will happen, not a picture of what might.**
4. **Ghost is unmistakably uncommitted, and banded/four-stop throughout (G2).** A previewed verdict is a verdict — it renders as the four-stop word, never a decimal; a previewed margin is a band. The ghost layer is visually distinct from committed state ("previewed — not applied"), so no reader can mistake a ghost for a fact (FR-003). G2 applies to hypotheticals with full force: a preview is not a licence to un-band.
5. **The preview pre-figures exactly the glow set (DEC-34 consistency).** The set the preview highlights is the set that will glow on commit — the preview and the glow are the same value-keyed diff computed at two times. This is not a coincidence to be maintained; it is the *definition* of the preview (rule 3), so DEC-34's "glow never under/over-reports" carries to the ghost for free.

### 2.2 Why the fork is cheap — immutability as the enabling property

The store keeps only canonical strings keyed by content hash and parses a fresh copy on every GET (SPEC-01): no caller holds a reference into stored state, and there is no interior mutability to deep-copy around. A fork is therefore a shallow copy of three append-only containers — the hash→canonical map, the logical-id→lineage index, the trace edge list, the delta array — plus fresh service instances wired over them. For the Meridian tableau (≈ 20 knowledge objects, a few hundred edges) this is microseconds, and it is *structurally* faithful: the fork runs the identical service code over identical store semantics, so there is no divergence surface between "preview" and "commit" to audit. The spec's phrase "an overlay of uncommitted objects over the real store" is one valid mechanism; a full clone is the *most obviously* honest one (no read-through fall-through logic to get wrong) and, at this scale, free. The note chooses the clone.

### 2.3 The edge cases, decided

- **Preview of a refusing act** — the refusal *is* the preview. The shadow snapshot carries the refusal banner (a `contested_knowledge` compile refusal, an `encoding_violation` on a dishonest band); the ghost renders it in place. Arming teaches the firewall; nothing is committed.
- **Preview atop a stale/mixed-stamp view** — the comparability guard runs first (it is part of the same snapshot). A ghost never renders over greyed, incomparable cells; the shadow snapshot inherits the guard's refusal exactly as the committed one would.
- **Two armed previews** — v1 permits **one** armed act at a time (stated on the surface). Arming a second cancels the first visibly. (Composing two shadow acts is a well-defined fork-of-a-fork, but presenting the sum honestly — which of two edits caused which cell to move — is a second legibility problem the slice does not take on. Deferred, stated.)
- **Cancel** — discards the fork; a no-op on committed state, guaranteed byte-identical by rule 2.

### 2.4 The register candidate — the preview is a new *read* class

The preview is not a stored object and not a delta, but it *is* a new kind of interaction — "compute what a write would change, show it, commit nothing" — with a genuine honesty rule (**previewed = computed, never estimated**). That is register content (a new interaction class the constitution should bless), so it is flagged as a candidate (concept §6.29), not asserted (DEC-2). Its subject is the app-layer `PreviewState` (`{armed_act, shadow_result, ghost_diff}`), never stored, never stamped. SPEC-25 may not be cited as the authority for it.

## 3. The verdict legend, decided (US1) — the CRT laid open, oracle-derived

**Decision.** The legend renders the four-stop mapping as **regions of the signed-margin sign rule** — robust `m_lo > 0`, marginal `m_lo = 0 ≤ m_hi`, tight `m_lo < 0 ≤ m_hi`, violated `m_hi < 0` — illustrated by the **frozen oracle O-3 sweep**: the band `[9,13]` against `at_most T` swept `T ∈ {8…14}`, whose verdict sequence is `[violated, tight, tight, tight, tight, marginal, robust]`, changing **only at the band edges 9 and 13, never inside** (vignette §9, `tests/oracle.test.ts`). The `marginal`-is-edge-coincidence disclosure is footnoted: `marginal` fires only when `m_lo = 0` exactly — the worst-case margin sits *on* the line — which in a continuous domain is a **measure-zero** coincidence of band edge and threshold; it is the boundary verdict, never an interior transition.

Three sub-decisions:

- **Oracle-derived, not live-derived.** The legend's illustration is computed from the *frozen* O-3 constants (`value = [9,13]`, `comparator = at_most`, the sweep set) through the same `marginBand`/`verdictFor` mapping the oracle pins — never from whatever world is loaded. A change to the illustration is therefore a register/oracle matter, not a data event (FR-001, US1 AS-2). The legend *shows the mapping's honesty property* (edges-only changes) rather than asserting it: the reader watches the sweep hold constant across the band interior.
- **It renders a mapping, never a score (DEC-19).** The four regions are labelled by the sign condition, not by any number "how robust"; there is no scale, no percentage, no distance-from-threshold headline. This is the CRT contract: a lookup, inspectable, with nothing hidden and nothing summed.
- **Reachable wherever a verdict renders, keyed in `legends.ts`.** The legend is one act away from every surface that shows a four-stop word (the matrix, the scenario strip, the DSM, the relaxation cards, the sensitivity table, the COA verdicts) — mounted alongside the existing per-component legend, and its copy lives in the canonical pill dictionary. "Why this word?" is always answerable in one click.

The legend obeys `data-projection="wall"` sizing like every component (SPEC-17 wall mode); it is display-only and never participates in content addressing.

## 4. Relaxation cards v2, decided (US3) — the risk residue the sacrifice set hides

**Decision.** Each relaxation card gains a **derived second line, "puts at risk"** — the commitments the candidate degrades *short of violation* relative to a stated **incumbent plan**, computed as verdict deltas over the same R3m world (e.g. `C3 robust → marginal`) — beneath the `sacrificed` headline; and the card body renders as **numbered, individually-traceable reasons** (the matrix-game move form: a claim, then enumerated reasons each opening its own trace).

Sub-decisions:

- **The `sacrificed` semantics do not change (SPEC-09 held).** `sacrificed` still means *exactly* the `violated` set — the strongest claim, computed by the scorer (note 04 §2). The at-risk line is **additive presentation**: `tight`/`marginal` were always "risks, not sacrifices" (note 04), and the review's §3.3 finding is that the card *under-describes* by showing only the violated set. v2 shows the residue; it does not relabel it. A commitment can never appear in both lines (violated ⇒ sacrificed; degraded-but-not-violated ⇒ at-risk).
- **The incumbent is stated, never assumed.** The at-risk line is a *delta*, so it needs a baseline. The baseline is the plan the commander would otherwise run — the canned **P1** (the strait-early COA in force), scored under the *same R3m world* as the candidates (apples to apples; the relaxation is happening in R3m). The comparison is derived by the reused SPEC-07 scorer, never authored. Where the incumbent cannot be scored (absent, refuses), the at-risk basis renders as a **stated absence**, never a silent empty line. The verdict order for "worse than" is the honest four-stop decisiveness order fixed by the oracle O-4 property test (`robust` best, then `marginal`, then `tight`, then `violated` worst) — the same order note 03 §3 and `tests/oracle.test.ts` already pin; no new ordering is invented.
- **Numbered reasons are the move form, traceable (G3).** The claim is the candidate's command-language narrative; the enumerated reasons are its sacrifices (each "gives up …") followed by its at-risk degradations (each "… slips robust → tight"), every reason carrying its commitment id so the existing one-hop trace menu opens its chain. No decimal, no margin on the card face (G2) — the banded margin stays in the trace drawer.

US3 is register-neutral: it re-renders scorer output (SPEC-07/09), asserts no new decision. The at-risk derivation is a fold over verdicts of the same class the matrix already shows.

## 5. The challenge affordance, decided (US4) — computed sensitivity, scoped to a verdict

**Decision.** "Challenge" on a verdict `(plan, commitment)` **re-renders the SPEC-11 sensitivity ranking restricted to that commitment**: the knowledge items whose band-edge perturbation *changes that verdict* (its perturbed verdict differs from its baseline at that commitment's index), each shown with its `single_source` flag, deep-linked to its S1 row where the lifecycle acts (contest, collect, refresh) live. No new computation: the challenge runs *the same* `SensitivityService.analyse` call the sensitivity panel runs (same inputs ⇒ same stamp, G1), then projects the ranking onto one commitment.

Sub-decisions:

- **It routes challenge, it does not adjudicate it** (the Red Teaming Handbook's binding warning). The affordance surfaces *which knowledge, pushed to its band edge, flips this verdict* — a computed key-assumptions check (the review's M8) — and hands the reader to the knowledge row. It never asserts "this assumption is wrong"; it names the load-bearing, uncorroborated inputs and steps aside.
- **The insensitive case is a first-class answer, never padded.** A verdict no single band-edge movement flips renders the honest sentence — "no single band-edge movement flips this verdict" — which is *itself assurance* (the verdict is robust to each assessment's stated uncertainty), not a blank or an apology. This is the direct counter to lip-service challenge: the honest "nothing flips it" is content.
- **Scope is the existing ranking, not a re-computation.** Restricting to the commitment index is a projection over the SPEC-11 `baseline_verdicts`/`perturbed_verdicts` vectors (commitment-ordered by the scorer's canonical sort). The commitment index is read off the same canonical order the scorer uses. Nothing is re-scored beyond the one sensitivity call the panel already makes; the stamp matches an equivalent sensitivity call exactly (US4 Independent Test).

US4 is register-neutral: it re-renders SPEC-11 output. No schema, no seam, no new stamp semantics.

## 6. Per-role action menus, decided (US5) — C2 role separation made legible

**Decision.** Each tab presents its **legal verbs** as a menu, from a single source-of-truth role→verb map, glossed and legend-keyed: **J-2** — create · contest · resolve · supersede · collect-mark; **planner (J-3/5)** — compile · generate-handful · relax · score; **commander** — select · waive; **observer** — none (read and trace only); the **Spatial · COA** surface is a *cross-role surface* (DEC-32's four role tabs stand unextended), so its menu names the authoring inputs it already exposes (move-waypoint, shift-window — planner-class writes) and marks itself a surface, not a role. No verb's semantics change; the shell arranges what the seam already permits (DEC-33).

Sub-decisions, decided against the two failure modes:

- **Menus reorganise, never restrict.** Every act previously reachable stays reachable; the menu is an *additive* legibility layer over the existing controls (resolve/reopen/supersede are already wired; the menu names the full legal set and routes the wired ones). Nothing is silently removed (FR-006, US5 Independent Test).
- **Deferred/automatic verbs are labelled honestly, never faked (DEC-4).** Some legal verbs are exercised *automatically* by the live pipeline (compile/score/generate/relax run on every recompute, not on a button); one — `select` (`POST /select`) — is **not yet built** (the DSM surface already states "no `/select` selection exists"). The menu therefore annotates each verb with *how it is exercised in this demonstrator*: live-actionable (a wired control), pipeline-automatic (runs on recompute), or deferred (labelled, never a dead button pretending to work). Presenting a `select` button that did nothing would be theatre; naming `select` as the commander's legal-but-unbuilt verb is honest C2 structure.
- **Observer read-only is the load-bearing assertion.** The observer tab exposes **no** write verb — the bridge watches the seam, it does not touch it. This is the C2 point the menus make legible: who may do what is business-process structure, and today it is implicit in which controls happen to be wired.

US5 is register-neutral **iff** it is pure re-arrangement (the spec's condition). It is: the menu adds no write surface — it re-presents existing, gated affordances and *documents* the legal set. The candidate is flagged conservatively (concept §6.30) only because "a per-role action menu as a standing shell element" is a shell-structure claim of DEC-32's class; the note's finding is that the behaviour needs no new decision (it refines DEC-32/33, does not fork them), and the candidate records that finding for ratification rather than asserting it.

## 7. What this slice does **not** do (deferred, stated)

- **No `/select`** — the commander's `select` verb is named-but-unbuilt (§6); the DSM's "no selection exists" statement stands. `/select` is a later slice.
- **No seam-level preview contract** — v1 previews client-side (§2); a `POST /preview` movement type is a later candidate if a second consumer appears.
- **No composed multi-arm preview** — one armed act at a time (§2.3); honest attribution of two simultaneous edits is deferred.
- **No new scalar, anywhere** — the whole slice re-renders bands, four-stop words, step counts, and classification words; it introduces no score, total, weight, or urgency number (DEC-19, FR-007). This is the invariant the component tests assert structurally (no forbidden numeric headline in any rendered surface).

## 8. The Meridian exhibit this slice must reproduce (the pinned checks)

- **US1** — the legend renders four regions with the sign conditions and the O-3 illustration `[9,13]` sweeping `at_most T` over `8…14` → `[violated, tight×4, marginal, robust]`, the marginal footnote present, all derived from the frozen constants; the legend appears on every verdict surface (structural check over the rendered app).
- **US2** — arming the K12a-resolve preview yields a ghost-diff set equal to the glow set the same resolve produces on commit (byte-equal); `arm → cancel` leaves store size, delta count, and every stamp byte-identical; arming a dishonest K3 band edit previews its `encoding_violation` refusal and commits nothing.
- **US3** — the R3m cards carry pinned at-risk lines derived from P1-under-R3m as the incumbent (whatever the scorer returns, pinned once observed), with `sacrificed` still exactly the violated set and each numbered reason trace-linked.
- **US4** — challenging a C4 verdict surfaces exactly the SPEC-11 contributors for C4, matching the sensitivity call's stamp; on Meridian under BASE the honest C4 flipper is **K6** (the documented band-propagation, SPEC-18) — the plans route clear of the threatened water, so K8 (the fire-control threshold) is genuinely insensitive there and surfacing it would be false (K8 becomes load-bearing under the threat scenarios, not BASE). The `single_source` flag is co-displayed for any single-source contributor (K8's flag is demonstrated on the ranking and the component); a robust-to-perturbation verdict renders the honest insensitive sentence.
- **US5** — each tab's menu equals its documented legal verb set; the observer tab exposes no write; no previously-wired act is removed.
