# Research note 07 — The system-flow view (S4 grown up)

Stage 7 (Surfaces & narratives) · per ASSAY-DEC-11 · 2026-07-13 · opened **ahead of Stages 5–6 under delegated authority** (concept §6.15) · bounded to hours, one page preferred
Prompts (from `assay-flow-infographic-spec.md` §6/§11): the **interaction/animation approach** for showing information flow to four audiences at once; the **fan-out-vs-recompute distinction** (how to render an instant read differently from a stamped recompute); the **auto-recompute honesty question** (is auto-triggering recompute defensible against the S2 "recompile when ready" stance?). Output feeds the flow-infographic build slice (the interactive view proposed by the spec; SPEC-14 site/gallery neighbourhood or its own SPEC-##).

## 1. Most of this view needs no research — three interaction questions do

The mechanics the flow view renders are already researched and built: compile (note 02), score and the four-stop verdict mapping (note 03), relaxation (note 04). The objects, the seven typed connectors, the six gates, the banded-honesty rendering, and the tour's spine (the walkthrough, itself the JP 2-01.3 JIPOE-supports-planning cycle played end-to-end) are all decided in the canonical set. This note deliberately spends its hours only on what is genuinely open: **how to structure one view for four audiences, how to render the flow legibly, and whether auto-recompute is honest.** Everything else the view does is a projection of settled machinery (ui-design §1.4).

## 2. The three zoom layers are a settled HCI result, not a house preference

The spec's L0/L1/L2 progressive disclosure (spec §4.1) is a direct instance of Shneiderman's **Visual Information-Seeking Mantra — "Overview first, zoom and filter, then details-on-demand"** (Shneiderman, 1996). His taxonomy names seven tasks (overview, zoom, filter, details-on-demand, relate, history, extract); the flow view uses exactly the first four. This matters for the "one artefact, four audiences" claim (spec §1): it is not four infographics awkwardly merged, it is the *standard* overview→detail progression, where a non-specialist stays at **overview (L0)**, a commander/SME works at **zoom-and-filter (L1)**, and a technical reviewer takes **details-on-demand (L2)** — the trace drawer opening a single node's edges. Munzner's *Visualization Analysis and Design* (2014) frames the same as overview+detail with the explicit warning that detail must be *reachable*, not *permanent* — which is why L2 is drill-in, not a fourth always-on panel that would drown L0's audience. The register-decided verdict is: the zoom control **is** the audience switch, and the literature says that is the right shape.

## 3. The flow *is* a provenance graph — so "why?" is a standard query, not a bespoke visual

ASSAY's trace graph (knowledge → channel → verdict → rationale, with typed edges written at compute time by the computing service — DEC-5, G3) is a **provenance graph** in the precise sense of the W3C PROV data model (PROV-DM, W3C Recommendation, 2013): *entities* (the stored objects), *activities* (compile, score, relax), and *agents* (the named owners), connected by `wasDerivedFrom` / `wasGeneratedBy` / `wasAttributedTo` / `wasRevisionOf`. ASSAY's edges map onto PROV almost one-for-one:

| ASSAY edge | PROV relation | Consequence for the view |
|---|---|---|
| `supersedes` | `wasRevisionOf` | K9→K5 is a revision, not a deletion — the tour animates it as such |
| `compiled_into`, `scored_from` | `wasGeneratedBy` an activity, `wasDerivedFrom` its inputs | the L2 backward chain is a transitive `wasDerivedFrom` closure |
| `cited_in` | `wasInfluencedBy` | the rationale cites the verdicts it rests on |
| named owner (K8 · J-2) | `wasAttributedTo` an agent | **G3's "terminates in a named owner" is exactly `wasAttributedTo`** |

The pay-off: the "why?" chain (G3, spec §7 read paths) is a standard provenance query — a backward transitive closure terminating in an agent attribution — not a bespoke diagram to be hand-maintained. It also tells us the rendering: node-link is the right idiom **at this scale** (a bounded tableau — one heartbeat on Meridian), where provenance-visualization practice favours node-link for small graphs and reserves aggregation/matrix forms for large ones. The frozen D+2/step-8 tableau (vignette §8) is small by construction, so node-link holds; a real dense world would not, which is a reason the view stays a *bounded* explainer, not a live operational graph.

## 4. Auto-recompute is honest for an *explainer* — because the protected property is attribution, not manual triggering

This is the one genuinely new call. S2 (the planner surface) uses **"recompile when ready"**: a supersession *flags* affected verdicts and the planner decides when to recompute (ui-design §3.4.4). The flow view proposes **auto-recompute** (spec §2.2, concept §6.15 candidate a). Do these contradict?

They do not, once two properties the reactive-systems literature keeps separate are named:

- **Consistency / glitch-freedom.** Bainomugisha et al. (2013) define a *glitch* as "a momentary view of inconsistent program state," and *glitch-freedom* as never exposing such a transient during recomputation. This is a **hard requirement** for the flow view regardless of trigger: when a recompute runs, the view must never show a half-updated matrix (old stamp on some cells, new on others). The comparability guard (G1, `stamp_mismatch`) is precisely this — it greys cross-stamp comparisons rather than render an inconsistent mix. So glitch-freedom is *inherited from G1*, not a new obligation.
- **Agency.** *Who decides to advance the state.* This is what "recompile when ready" is about, and the reason it lives on S2 is specific: on S2 a recompute **changes the comparison basis under a live handful** — it has a commitment consequence (the planner may want to hold `7f3a…c91` while briefing, or refer back to the J-2 rather than accept K9). The planner owns that decision because the planner owns the plan.

The flow view is an **explainer, not an operational planning surface** — nobody commits a plan off it, no handful is live under it. The *agency* concern that motivates "recompile when ready" on S2 therefore does not transfer. What must transfer is **attribution**: the honesty claim the whole demonstrator rests on is that a value never changes *unaccountably*. The walkthrough's normative line is "nothing recomputes **silently**" — silently, not automatically. Auto-recompute keeps attribution mandatory (spec §2.2): the stamp badge flips, a delta row lands, a gate pulse names the trigger. A viewer can always answer "why did this number change?" — the delta feed says so, by name and op.

**Conclusion.** Auto-recompute (attribution-visible, glitch-free via G1) is honest *for the explainer*, and it does **not** assert any change to S2's "recompile when ready" — that interaction is unchanged and unreplaced (the flow view is S4's explainer form, not a replacement for S2). The genuinely dangerous variant is the deferred one: a truly-silent "you-are-the-optimiser" toggle drops attribution, crosses the line the walkthrough draws, and is rightly held for an SME to react to before it hardens (DEC-27; concept §6.15 candidate d).

## 5. The sandbox drives the real seam — and labels what it cannot yet compute

DEC-4's invariant is blunt: "the scorer is honestly real even in the mock… sensitivity, discrimination, and robustness demos are re-scoring loops and become **theatre over a fake scorer**." The sandbox therefore calls the built services (spec §7) — `CompileService`, `ScoreService`, `RelaxService`, `KnowledgeService`, `DeltaLog`, `TraceStore` — so consequences are computed, not tweened. But two computations the view wants are **not built yet** (both `not-started` in `status.yml`): the **staleness fan-out** (thesis F, Stage 6) and full **scenario-robustness** strip fidelity (Stage 5). The honest resolution follows directly from DEC-4: a *scripted* result is acceptable **iff it is visibly labelled as scripted and is oracle-consistent** — the line DEC-4 draws is between *theatre that pretends to compute* and an *honest reconstruction that says it is one*. So: the **tour** replays the walkthrough's oracle-consistent results (scripted by design, and labelled so); the sandbox's supersede-fan-out and full R-strip **gate on Stages 5–6** and, until then, either stay disabled or render the walkthrough's known result behind an explicit "scripted — not yet computed" marker. A scripted number dressed as a computed one is the exact failure DEC-4 names; a labelled one is honest.

## What we will do differently

1. **Build the three zoom layers as Shneiderman's mantra** (overview / zoom-and-filter / details-on-demand). "One view, four audiences" is the standard overview→detail progression, not four builds; L2 is drill-in (reachable), never a permanent panel (Munzner).
2. **Render the flow as the trace graph read as a PROV provenance graph.** The "why?" chain (G3) is a backward `wasDerivedFrom` closure terminating in `wasAttributedTo` (a named owner) — a standard query, not a hand-maintained diagram. Node-link idiom, justified by the bounded tableau scale; the boundedness is load-bearing, not incidental.
3. **Allow auto-recompute in the flow view, attribution-visible and glitch-free.** The protected property is **attribution, not manual triggering**: keep stamp-flip + delta row + gate pulse mandatory, inherit glitch-freedom from the G1 comparability guard, and leave S2's "recompile when ready" agency interaction unchanged and unreplaced. Hold the truly-silent toggle for an SME (it drops attribution).
4. **Drive the sandbox against the real seam (DEC-4); label, never fake, what is not yet computed.** Staleness fan-out (Stage 6) and full robustness strip (Stage 5) are unbuilt — the tour replays oracle-consistent results marked as scripted; the computed sandbox variants gate on those stages. A scripted result must never masquerade as a computed one.
5. **Reuse the shipped components and ship self-contained** (spec §4.5/§4.6) — banded honesty inherited, embed offline-clean, one component in two homes.

This note asserts **no new register decision.** The zoom, provenance, and attribution findings *ground* the concept §6.15 candidates — they explain why the candidates are honest and buildable — but they do not originate or ratify them; §4's argument is offered for the ratifying batch to weigh, not asserted as settled. Any latitude the build spends against those candidates is within the delegated authority recorded at concept §6.15 (DEC-2's escape hatch), and DEC-11 is satisfied by this note preceding implementation.
