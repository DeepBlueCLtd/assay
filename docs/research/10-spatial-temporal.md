# Research note 10 — Spatial & temporal COA visualisation (the map that must not lie)

SPEC-19 (front-end depth lane, post-SPEC-16) · per ASSAY-DEC-11 · 2026-07-15 · bounded to hours, one page preferred
Prompts (from `specs/019-coa-visualisation/spec.md` US1/US2): how a **banded channel surface** renders without implying a point value; how the **temporal dimension** renders (validity windows, task windows, the storm surge) without tweening certainty; which uncertainty-visualisation and cartographic conventions this domain can honestly borrow; **≥3 alternative perspectives** appraised for false-precision risk, one recommended that reveals what the tables hide. Output gates the SPEC-19 build (mockups + interaction) and closes with an explicit **invitation to review**, not a decision asserted as closed.

## 1. Most of this view needs no research — two rendering rules and one perspective choice do

The data is already spatial and temporal: regions are `VignetteConfig` bounding boxes on the 60×60 grid, routes are `plans.json` waypoints with `enter_step`/`exit_step` windows, and the compiled world is the sparse channel set (default band + time-boxed `RegionOverride`s, ASSAY-DEC-29). The map schematic itself is ratified (ASSAY-DEC-31(c), the flow view's static panel); score-time materialisation is a pure, already-shipped function (`channelAt(world, config, kind, x, y, t)` — note 03 §2). What is genuinely open is **(i)** how a band renders *as a band* on a surface, **(ii)** how validity and task windows render without interpolated certainty, and **(iii)** which perspective earns the pixels — the three concept §6 item 21 sub-questions this note grounds (never ratifies — DEC-2).

## 2. The banded-surface rule — an interval is not a distribution, and most of the literature assumes it is

The uncertainty-visualisation literature's strongest techniques assume a *known distribution*: hypothetical outcome plots animate draws from it (Hullman, Resnick & Adar 2015), quantile dotplots discretise it (Kay et al. 2016), gradient/violin fades encode its density (Correll & Gleicher 2014). An ASSAY `Band` has **no interior structure** — `[lo, hi]` bounds from an assessed source, with mean/mid/most-likely operations forbidden (DEC-15). Any encoding that fades, samples, or peaks *inside* the interval therefore asserts a density the source never gave — the exact false precision G2 exists to stop. Those techniques are **rejected**, not for weakness but for honesty: they answer a question ("how is probability distributed?") ASSAY refuses to invent an answer to.

What survives is the interval-honest subset, and cartography names it. Using Kinkeldey, MacEachren & Schiewe's (2014) taxonomy — *intrinsic* (vary the symbol itself) vs *extrinsic* (add a glyph), *coincident* (uncertainty on the value's own mark) vs *adjacent*: the banded surface uses a **coincident, intrinsic pair**:

1. **A region override fills with a two-stop encoding — the `lo` stop and the `hi` stop rendered as a split fill (paired swatches), never a single interpolated mid-stop.** A reader sees both endpoints of the band wherever they see the region at all; there is no single colour standing in for "the" value because there is no "the" value.
2. **Band *width* (`hi − lo`, a real quantity, not an invented one) drives a hatched overlay whose density grows with the width.** This borrows the *principle* of value-suppressing uncertainty palettes (Correll, Moritz & Heer 2018) — an uncertain cell must not look as confident as a certain one — while rejecting VSUP's mechanics, because a VSUP wedge keys its value axis to a single representative scalar per cell, which for an interval means choosing a midpoint (DEC-15 violation). Hatching for imprecision is itself a settled cartographic convention (MacEachren et al. 2005; Bonneau et al. 2014).
3. **Hover always yields the exact `[lo, hi] unit` through the shipped `bandPill`** — details-on-demand (Shneiderman 1996), inheriting the note 09 audit wholesale (deterministic construal, anchoring, false precision, endpoint-labelling bias all resisted by the pill's construction). Per that audit, the legend labels the two stops as the band's *bounds*, never as "best/worst" or "min/max" (Joslyn & Savelli 2010 — endpoint labelling re-introduces the deterministic construal).
4. **A degenerate band (`lo = hi`, e.g. K1's surveyed depth — an observed fact, DEC-14) may render as a single stop**; the split-fill rule binds *assessed* sources. A channel with only its quiet `default` and no override renders that default as a band across the extent — absence of override is not absence of value.

The doctrinal anchor is unchanged from note 02: the map is the honest analogue of ATP 2-01.3's **MCOO** — named areas with operational meaning overlaid on terrain — not a heat map. Regions are the first-class marks; the surface never pretends to per-cell knowledge the sparse world does not contain.

## 3. The temporal rule — render validity, never interpolation

The compiled world's only temporal structure is **discrete extents**: a `RegionOverride` is active on `[from_step, until_step]` (K5's surge band on steps 0–16, K9's on 8–36), a route leg occupies `[enter_step, exit_step]`. The rule follows:

1. **Windows render as bounded interval bars** (the Gantt idiom) on a shared step axis — channel validity windows, route task windows, and scenario excursion windows all on one clock. Between windows, and beyond a window's edge, the state renders as *unknown or lapsed* — visibly marked (thesis F), never carried forward as if current. The **supersession edge** (K5 → K9, cross-lineage DEC-21) is drawn where the windows overlap (steps 8–16, where materialisation's documented tie order — later `from_step` wins — hands the cell to K9).
2. **No tweening.** A band valid on `[8, 36]` is *flat* on `[8, 36]` — the compiled surface carries no per-step variation, so the ridge renders as a rectangle (`[1.1, 1.8] m surge` × steps 8–36), not a curve. Animating or easing between two bands' values manufactures a smooth certainty trajectory no source stated — the temporal twin of the gradient-fill lie in §2.
3. **A stated peak is a quotation, not a curve.** K9's provenance note says "surge peaking D+5–D+7" — at 6 h/step, steps 20–28. That is *prose stated by METOC*, not a compiled value; it may render only as a **labelled annotation on the window (a marked sub-extent, attributed to its source)**, never as a synthesised per-step profile. The general rule: anything on the canvas is either a fixture value, a computed value, or a *visibly attributed quotation* — the flow view's "scripted — not yet computed" discipline (note 07 §5, DEC-4) applied to time.
4. The **space-time cube** (Hägerstrand 1970; Kraak 2003) is the classical unification of (x, y, t) and is exactly the shape of a route through time-boxed channels — but as a shipped 2-D-screen artefact it trades occlusion and projection ambiguity for the continuity it *implies* along the t-axis (a drawn 3-D trajectory reads as continuously known). It stays a background idiom here (see §4e); the map + timeline pair delivers the cube's content as two honest orthogonal cuts.

## 4. The perspective survey — what each view could lie about, and what it reveals

Appraised against the spec's §5 test: the pick must make the **SPEC-18 honest gap** visible (a band reaches a verdict only where the metric reads the channel **and** the route enters the region).

- **(a) Plan-view map** — regions as banded surfaces (§2), routes overlaid. *False-precision risk*: the single-stop heat fill (one colour = one value) and implied per-cell knowledge; defused by the §2 split-fill/hatch rule and region-first marks. *Reveals*: the geometry itself — `FE-ANVIL`'s track entering `fac_waters` and standing off `air_defence`; `FE-FALCON`'s position relative to `port_district`. Static geometry alone, though, does not show *when*, nor how much exposure was banked.
- **(b) Exposure-and-events timeline** — validity windows, task windows, and the storm window on one step axis (§3). *False-precision risk*: interpolated certainty curves between samples and silently-carried-forward lapsed bands; defused by the §3 window rule and lapse marking. *Reveals*: alignment — which knowledge was *in force* while each leg ran (K5 lapses at 16; K9 carries the surge window under both plans' middle game; `FE-ANVIL`'s P1 dwell at steps 8–9 sits inside K9's window and K6's un-windowed band).
- **(c) The exposure profile** *(the genuinely new cross-cut)* — a plan's banded `threat_exposure` **accumulating along the route over time**: a staircase of `[lo, hi]` extents that steps **only at leg boundaries** (the metric is Σ legs of band × dwell — note 03 §2), flat between them, both bounds drawn. *False-precision risk*: a single accumulation line implying one true trajectory, or a slope implying continuous accrual mid-leg; defused by drawing the *band* of the partial sum as a stepped extent, never a line. *Reveals what no table can*: **C4 becomes legible as *where and when* the dwell was banked.** On the Meridian fixtures: P1 `FE-ANVIL` banks its entire C4 exposure — `[12, 36]` band-hours against a threshold of 12 (the *tight* verdict's whole substance) — in **one 6-hour dwell at (32, 22) inside `fac_waters` at steps 8–9**, under K6's `[2, 6]` sorties/day band; the profile is `[0, 0]` everywhere else. `air_defence` contributes **nothing** — K7's region is never entered, so K7's band never reaches C4 (the route-miss, shown as a region the staircase never rises in). And C3 has *no profile at all* — `civil_harm_exposure` counts `FE-FALCON` legs inside `port_district` (P1: 0, P2: 1) and never reads the `civil_density` band; K3's `[35 000, 55 000]` is compiled but geometrically unconsumed (the honest gap SPEC-18 proved and ASSAY-DEC-35 affirmed as a design feature).
- **(d) Handful small-multiples** — the four generated plans over one map. *False-precision risk*: side-by-side maps invite reading visual area/track-length as a scalar score, quietly re-ranking what DEC-19 forbids ranking; defusable but the reveal (the trade axes read spatially) is already carried by the handful strip's `distinct_because` plus perspective (a). Deferred, not built.
- **(e) Space-time cube** — (x, y, t) in one artefact. *False-precision risk*: implied continuity along t and occlusion-driven misreading (§3.4). High cost, and its content is exactly recoverable from (a) + (b). Rejected as a shipped view.

**Recommendation: the composed pair (a) + (b) — map and timeline on one shared state — with (c), the exposure profile, embedded as the connective tissue between them** (the C4 staircase rendered under the timeline's step axis, its risers keyed to the map's regions). This is the recommendation because it satisfies the §5 test *by construction*: the named exhibit — **K6→C4 propagation as a `[12, 36]` band-hour riser inside `fac_waters` at steps 8–9; K7→C4 as the `air_defence` box the staircase never rises in; K3→C3 as a count with no band read at all** — ties directly to thesis A (the pipeline, spatially legible end-to-end), thesis E (K6's wide band is *load-bearing* exactly at that riser), and thesis F (the K5/K9 supersession edge on the same clock), i.e. the §7 coverage rows SPEC-18 traced in prose.

## 5. Interaction — understanding is selection; input is authorship; neither touches the answer

- **Scrub/hover (understanding)** is pure *selection over the compiled world*: the step slider evaluates `channelAt(·, ·, ·, x, y, t)` and the leg-active predicate at step *t* — the same pure materialisation the scorer uses, no recompute, no new state (Shneiderman's zoom/filter + details-on-demand). Hover payloads render through `bandPill`/`provenanceChip`, so every surfaced value arrives banded and attributed.
- **Drag/shift (input)** never edits an answer: dragging a waypoint or shifting a task window **authors a new `Plan` version** (immutable objects, DEC-20/21) and hands it to the *real* SPEC-16 in-browser pipeline — compile unchanged, `ScoreService.score()` fresh over the edited plan — with the glow diff (G6, value-keyed) marking exactly the panels whose displayed value moved. Byte-identical to a from-scratch run (G1). An infeasible or dishonest edit refuses in place (`refusalBanner`, G2/G5); nothing persists.
- **The DEC-31(d) caution binds**: recompute-on-drop is attribution-visible (stamp flip + glow + the edited plan version named), never silent optimisation; no affordance may search plan-space on the operator's behalf. **Promotion to a standing surface (a fifth tab or S2/S4 mode) is explicitly deferred to after the SME review below** — concept §6 item 21(d).

## What we will do differently

1. **Render every assessed band as a two-stop split fill + width-keyed hatching; exact `[lo, hi] unit` on hover via `bandPill`; no single-stop fill for an assessed channel, ever** (§2 — the banded-surface rule; concept §6.21(a) grounded).
2. **Render time as discrete validity/task windows with lapse and supersession marked; no tweening, no interpolated certainty; stated peaks render only as attributed quotations** (§3 — the temporal rule; §6.21(b) grounded).
3. **Build the map + timeline pair with the exposure profile as connective tissue**; the shipped exhibit is the honest gap as geometry — K6→C4's `[12,36]` riser in `fac_waters`, K7's untouched `air_defence`, K3's unread band (§4).
4. **Keep understanding as pure selection and input as plan-version authorship through the real pipeline** — recompute live, glow value-keyed, refusal in place, nothing silent (§5; §6.21(c) grounded).
5. **Mockups are projections of fixtures** (regions, routes, compiled bands — the SPEC-14 gallery's honesty class), self-contained and offline-clean (comms §6), dated-frozen, carrying the Meridian "assessment, not fact" disclaimer.
