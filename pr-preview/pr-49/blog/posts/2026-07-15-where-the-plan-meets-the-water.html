Where the plan meets the water

2026-07-15 · a COA as a route through a banded surface over time — and why the map is the most dangerous place in the whole demonstrator.

The previous article proved something in prose: a banded answer reaches a verdict only where the metric reads the channel and the route enters the region. K6's wide sortie-rate band propagates into C4 because TG ANVIL dwells in fac_waters; K7's air-defence envelope never reaches C4 because the route never enters air_defence; K3's census never reaches C3 at all, because that commitment is a geometric red line. Words, with numbers to check them. What nothing did was show it. Every ASSAY surface to date is a table — honest, but flat. A verdict is a route walking through a banded surface over time, and until now that geometry lived only in the reader's head.

So this slice draws it. And drawing it is exactly where the honesty rules earn their keep, because a map is the single most tempting place to launder banded intelligence into false precision. A heat-map cell wants one colour; a timeline wants a smooth curve; a route wants one cost. Every one of those is a scalar an assessed source never gave us.

The rules, decided before the pixels

Research note 10-spatial-temporal.md (the DEC-11 gate for this slice) decides two rendering rules against the uncertainty-visualisation literature — most of which, it turns out, assumes a probability distribution ASSAY refuses to invent. Hypothetical outcome plots, quantile dotplots, gradient fades: all encode a density inside the interval, and an ASSAY band has no inside — just [lo, hi] from an assessed source (DEC-15 forbids the midpoint). What survives is the interval-honest subset:

  1. A banded surface renders as a split fill — the band's lo stop and hi stop as hard halves, never one interpolated colour — with hatch density carrying the band's width and the exact [lo, hi] unit on hover, with its named source. Only an observed fact (lo = hi) earns a single stop.
  2. Time renders validity, never interpolation. Knowledge validity windows and route task windows are discrete extents on one clock; a lapsed channel is marked lapsed, never silently carried forward; and a stated peak — K9's "surge peaking D+5–D+7" — renders as a quoted, attributed annotation, never a synthesised curve, because the compiled band is flat and nothing honest can bend it.

Play with it

Try this

  1. Find the gap without being told. On the timeline, P1's exposure staircase rises exactly once — +[12, 36] band-hours where ANVIL dwells in fac_waters at steps 8–9 — and that riser is the whole substance of C4's tight verdict (threshold 12: the band straddles it). Now look at air_defence on the map: K7's band is compiled, banded, drawn — and the staircase never rises there, because no route enters it. That is the K6/K7 asymmetry as geometry.
  2. Drag the route and watch the verdict move. Grab ANVIL's waypoint at (32,22) and pull it west, out of fac_waters. That authors a new plan version (plans are immutable) and re-scores it through the same ScoreService the batch pipeline uses — C4 flips tight → robust, the margin band re-renders, and exactly the things whose value moved glow for ten seconds (G6). Nothing is tweened; the stamp on the panel is the recompute's receipt.
  3. Scrub the clock. The slider is selection, not simulation: it shows the compiled world at step n — which windows are live, which legs are active. Scrub past step 36 and the storm surface doesn't fade; it lapses, marked, back to the channel default. Notice nothing glows on a scrub: nothing changed, and the glow refuses to say otherwise.
  4. Try to lie to it. Set K6's band inverted (lo > hi), or assert K8's single-source repair assessment as a hard fact without its waiver. Both come back as refusals, in place, with nothing saved — the same encoding firewall that guards the batch pipeline guards the drag-and-edit surface.
Why "mockup, for review"? The research note ends with an invitation, not a verdict: is the spatial treatment honest and legible? is the temporal treatment honest and legible? Those two questions are printed on the page itself, and SME reactions (comms §12) decide whether this surface hardens into a standing home — a fifth tab or an S2/S4 mode — or gets reworked. The interaction model ships now so reviewers can feel the input; the promotion decision is deliberately held open (concept §6 item 21(d)).

One more honesty note, because it is the quiet heart of the slice: nothing on the map is drawn. Regions are the fixture bounding boxes; routes are the stated plans.json legs; every channel value is the compiled world materialised at a cell and step by the same function the scorer uses. The map is a projection of the store — the gallery's honesty class, with geometry.
